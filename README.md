# The Turing Program

The rapid deployment of large-scale artificial intelligence has outpaced the social and legal structures intended to govern them. The Turing Program is an initiative dedicated to bridging this gap. We are building a collaborative framework designed to move AI ethics from abstract principles into enforceable, technical standards.

Our mission is to ensure that transparency, accountability, and human agency are not treated as optional features, but as fundamental requirements for any system influencing human life.

---

## The Landscape of Necessity

The conversation around AI ethics is no longer purely philosophical. High-court rulings and legislative shifts across the globe have established that creators are responsible for the outcomes of their algorithms. We draw our initiative's urgency from these legal foundations:

### European Union: The Precedence of Rights

The EU has consistently prioritized the "dignity of the natural person" over algorithmic efficiency. In the landmark case **Schrems II**, the Court of Justice of the European Union (CJEU) reinforced that data protection must follow the data, regardless of where it is processed. Furthermore, the **EU AI Act** establishes a risk-based hierarchy, effectively codifying the idea that "black box" systems in high-risk sectors (like hiring or policing) are legally untenable if they cannot be audited.

### United States: The Doctrine of Algorithmic Disgorgement

In the US, the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has moved beyond mere fines. In cases like **FTC v. Kurbo (2022)** and **FTC v. Everalbum (2021)**, the commission required companies to not only delete illegally obtained data but to destroy the algorithms and models trained on that data. This creates a powerful legal precedent: an unethical foundation invalidates the entire technological asset.

### India: The Principle of Informational Autonomy

The Supreme Court of India, in **Justice K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India**, recognized the right to privacy as a fundamental right. This ruling has become the cornerstone for challenging automated decision-making. Recent interpretations suggest that when an AI system makes a life-altering decision without a "reasoned order," it may violate the constitutional guarantee of due process and equal protection under the law.

## Our Approach

Project Integrity focuses on three core pillars:

* **Verifiable Transparency:** Moving beyond "Open Source" to "Open Logic," where the decision-making weights of a model can be interpreted by third-party auditors.
* **Bias Mitigation Protocols:** Developing standardized stress tests for datasets to identify and neutralize socio-economic biases before training begins.
* **The Human-in-the-Loop Mandate:** Creating technical barriers that prevent autonomous systems from executing high-stakes actions without a meaningful human secondary review.
